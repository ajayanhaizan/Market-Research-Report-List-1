<p><p>The osteoarthritis pain medicine market is categorized into three main types: oral, injection, and external. Oral medications include nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and analgesics, which are taken to alleviate pain systemically. Injection medications, such as corticosteroids and hyaluronic acid, provide targeted relief by delivering treatments directly into the joint, offering quicker and more potent effects. External treatments encompass topical analgesics and patches that relieve pain through skin absorption, providing localized support without systemic side effects. Together, these options cater to diverse patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
