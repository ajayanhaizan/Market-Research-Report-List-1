<p><p>Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs) are the essential components in medications that provide therapeutic effects. The market for APIs is typically divided into two main types: synthetic APIs and biotech APIs. Synthetic APIs are chemically produced compounds, often characterized by their scalability and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for a wide range of generic drugs. In contrast, biotech APIs are derived from living organisms, including proteins and antibodies, and are primarily used in specialized treatments, such as biologics, targeting complex diseases.</p></p>
