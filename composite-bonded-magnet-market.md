<p><p>The composite bonded magnet market primarily employs two key manufacturing processes: injection molding and pressing molding. Injection molding involves melting a mixture of magnetic materials and polymers, which is then injected into a mold to create complex, precise shapes. This method offers high production rates and intricate designs. Conversely, pressing molding uses a powder form of the composite mixture, which is pressed into shape and then sintered. This technique is effective for producing dense magnets with specific magnetic properties, often used in various industrial applications.</p></p>
