<p><p>The knitted fabrics market is primarily divided into two types: weft knitted fabrics and warp knitted fabrics. Weft knitted fabrics are created by interlocking yarns horizontally, resulting in stretchable, comfortable materials commonly used in garments like t-shirts and sweaters. In contrast, warp knitted fabrics are made by looping yarns vertically, producing more stable and durable textiles, ideal for sportswear, lingerie, and technical applications. Each type serves distinct purposes and caters to varying consumer needs in the textile industry.</p></p>
