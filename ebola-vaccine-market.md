<p><p>The Ebola vaccine market comprises several types, including inactivated vaccines, DNA vaccines, virus vector vaccines, and others. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens to elicit an immune response without causing disease. DNA vaccines use genetically engineered DNA to produce antigens, prompting an immune reaction. Virus vector vaccines utilize harmless viruses to deliver Ebola genes, stimulating immunity. Other vaccine types may include subunit and mRNA vaccines, each offering unique mechanisms and benefits in combating Ebola outbreaks and enhancing public health preparedness.</p></p>
